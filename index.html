Dashboard Overview

Project Description

This project analyzes procurement performance across five suppliers and five item categories, tracking total spend, savings, average unit cost, and supplier compliance and quality metrics. The dashboard gives stakeholders a clear view of where money is being spent, which suppliers are pricing fairly, and where quality or delivery issues are putting the supply chain at risk. Turning raw procurement data into decisions around sourcing, negotiation, and supplier management.

Key Findings

  • Compliance is strong but not universal: 82% of orders met compliance standards, leaving an 18% non-compliance rate representing tangible supply chain risk.
  • Supplier quality varies sharply: Delta_Logistics handles the highest order volume but also carries one of the highest defective rates, while Alpha_Inc shows the lowest defect rate despite smaller volume.
  • Fulfillment is mostly reliable: 72% of orders were fully delivered, but the combined 17% in "Pending" and "Partially Delivered" status signals room to tighten supplier delivery SLAs.
  • MRO and Office Supplies dominate volume, accounting for the largest share of total quantity (188K and 180K units) making them the highest-leverage categories for cost negotiation.
  • Avg. unit cost sits at $53.7 across all suppliers a useful negotiation benchmark for flagging which suppliers are pricing way above or below the norm on a per-unit basis.
  • Savings are seasonal: monthly savings spike sharply in March ($474,877) then dip in May ($221,421), indicating inconsistent savings capture likely tied to bulk-order timing or contract renewal cycles.

Recommendations

  • Prioritize quality audits with Delta_Logistics given its combination of high volume and elevated defect rate.
  • Investigate the 18% non-compliance segment to determine whether it's concentrated in specific suppliers or categories.
  • Smooth savings capture by renegotiating contract timing so cost-avoidance isn't concentrated in a single month.
  • Use MRO and Office Supplies as leverage points for consolidated supplier negotiations given their volume share.
  • Benchmark each supplier's negotiated price against the $53.7 average unit cost to identify which contracts are overdue for renegotiation.
